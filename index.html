Self-reflection

Over the past for weeks I have completed a range of tasks, each of these tasks have led me to exercise different parts of both my brain and my creative process. When we were given the first task, I was immediately thinking of different themes to limit the creations too such as; using only set colours, picking a medium, picking a theme or subject in the end I did not settle for anything and tried to do something different every day. I still kept drawing as a general fallback if I ran out of time at the end of the day. Throughout the week especially the first few days I would have the daily creation on my mind quite a bit, thinking of what I could make that day. Having this mindset was quite interesting I found myself looking at different subjects or objects and thinking ‘what could I do with this’ or how could I incorporate it or run with it to become a more fully-fledged idea. However, much of this thinking didn’t resolve into a daily creation though it was still good to observe.

 

The second weekly task was possibly the most intensive as once one starts to consider all of the interactions that they encounter in a day they may soon be overwhelmed. After this thought though and the rationalisation that you touching the carpet on the floor is not a valid interaction I set about recording the many interactions that I had with technology and people. I found the accurate recording of interactions to be quite hard as when using a pen make a quick note of some interactions, I would have to note that I used a pen, and this was emphasised even more when documenting on to a word doc. The many interactions that take place seamlessly are also hard to note and were at time missed, interactions such as talking to a family member happen so often you barely known you’ve spoken. A considerable revelation I had while completing this task was when I considered our interactions with the immediate environment around us, for example I was sitting in my backyard and a bird was singing and I was listening. This may not be strictly an interaction, but it got me thinking about how things interact with us and not just us with them.

 

The final tasks had me question my thinking once again as I considered if a machine learning software could be considered creative. For this I was a torn as there are things that the software could do that were more creative and some that were less. One of the main things I tried to stay aware of is our subconscious application of meaning onto a painting or artwork as described in Bodens readings from class. This saw myself be quite harsh on reviewing the software and I think that if some sort of blind test was done to review only the artworks there would be many different things said. For the last task my conceptual thinking was quite limited, in trying to find primary information about a product/technology I tended to just go with what I could find. The task itself did get me thinking about a variety of ways it could be completed. I was thinking that if you choose a technology that liked and could not find primary sources of information you could conduct your own usability testing on an interactive tech to determine its user experience. This would have been fun to do depending on your int. tech but could also be some tiresome work when there are great sources online. Overall I think these tasks got me thinking, investigating and exercising my critical skills through the variety of the activities and the investigative nature they promote.
